The Washington Conference

1921



(Harris)
This Image shows a meeting of important international powers in Washington. It was held to address an issue that the American Government held with Japan's military at that point, namely that it was growing too large and powerful. It led to the signing of the Washington Conference, a trade agreement that limited the number of war ships Japan could produce. This restriction, amoung other factors, caused a build up of resentment against the United states, potentially connected to Japan's initial strike against Pearl Harbor.
